2026-07-10

WhackSmacker status: cleaner navigation, localized UI, and Korean Chapter 40

Human Voice

Today the CLI got polished a bit more and some language chapters were added. For Korean we're at chapter 40, and it seems that we're getting into high beginner territory. Also implemented is Traditional Chinese as one of the UI languages, but a lot of polishing is needed still.

Technical update

The latest WSM work brings a cleaner terminal layout, the first app-level source-language choices, more useful review-deck status feedback, and Korean content through Chapter 40. It remains a pre-alpha project, and the Traditional Chinese UI still needs polishing.

Cleaner CLI navigation

The CLI/TUI now uses a cleaner three-pane layout: left navigation, a middle Output pane, and a right Toggles pane. The Toggles pane currently holds an orange source-language toggle showing only English and 中文（臺灣）. Left and right movement switches directly between navigation and Toggles, skipping Output. The old source-language entry has been removed from the left-pane Settings menu.

English and Traditional Chinese UI options

English (en-US) and Traditional Chinese for Taiwan (zh-Hant-TW) are now supported as app/source-language UI options. App strings use the selected language with fallback, and package, content, and review metadata can provide localized source-content values. Existing plain English strings remain valid and fall back correctly. This is app and metadata infrastructure, not a full curriculum translation, and the Traditional Chinese UI still has plenty of rough edges.

Review decks show their state

Review decks now use visible colors for status: magenta for not started, yellow when cards are available, blue when no cards are due, and green when finished. The output pane can report There are <number> cards to review., and deck status updates live as review progress changes.

Korean reaches Chapter 40

Korean content now reaches Chapter 40, moving from absolute beginner toward high beginner territory. Chapters 31–40 use the newer structure: odd chapters are dialogues, even chapters are narratives, and every chapter introduces two grammar points with one serving as a connector or linking point. Sentences are also becoming longer and more varied while earlier grammar continues to return naturally.

Earlier expansion also added the English module, extended multiple languages through their early chapter blocks, and moved Vietnamese further ahead than most of the other language tracks.

Curriculum rules are harder to forget

The odd/even rule is now explicit and checked in generated packages and tests: odd chapters are dialogues and even chapters are narratives. Normal review-card examples also remain strict: each example must be a literal learner-facing read-content sentence, and examples render as bullets.
